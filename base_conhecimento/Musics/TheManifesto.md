# The Manifesto

Featuring:
Tueno & Proof.

“The Manifesto” is the second single for Gorillaz’s ninth studio album, “The Mountain.” The song likely originates from the sessions for “Demon Days” as a verse from the late artist Proof is present. A snippet of the song was found on the Kong Studios website on October 6th, 2025. It subsequently released two days later.

The album's second single, "The Manifesto", features late rapper Proof, whom the band had worked with on "911" in 2001, as well as Argentinian rapper Trueno, with Proof's contributions believed to be an unused freestyle from those sessions.[36] The song also features an uncredited appearance from flautist Ajay Shankar Prasanna. Prasanna, who recorded his contributions in New Delhi, chose to play Dhani and Malkauns ragas on the song.[37] In an interview with Zane Lowe on Beats 1, Albarn and Hewlett revealed that the song's name is in part a reference to the band's original manifesto, in which Albarn and Hewlett had specified that the band's drummer, Russel Hobbs, would be able to call on the spirits of deceased musicians to collaborate with.[38] The song also features contributions brothers Amaan Ali Bangash and Ayaan Ali Bangash, the Jea Band Jaipur, and the Mountain Choir.
